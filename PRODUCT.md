product

## Users

Home espresso enthusiasts who have installed GaggiMate smart controls in their Gaggia espresso machines. Users range from technically capable hobbyists to baristas who want precise control over brew temperature, pressure, and timing. They interact with the machine daily, often in a morning routine with limited time.

## Product Purpose

GaggiMate provides real-time monitoring and control of an espresso machine through an embedded LVGL display and web UI. The primary workflow is: select a profile, start a brew, monitor temperature/pressure via the ring gauge, and receive feedback when done. Success means consistent, repeatable shots with minimal intervention.

## Brand Personality

Precision, clarity, reliability. The aesthetic says "expert tool" not "consumer gadget." No unnecessary decoration. Every element earns its place. The Nothing theme specifically evokes the Nothing phone aesthetic: OLED black, stark contrast, minimal dot-matrix sensibility.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboards with card grids, blue/purple gradients, and rounded-corner excess. Consumer-grade appliances with cartoonish icons and playful colors. Any design that obscures critical brewing data behind decorative chrome.

## Design Principles

1. **Data clarity above all** — Temperature, pressure, and time are the sacred metrics. They must be readable at a glance from across a kitchen.
2. **Reliability signals trust** — The UI should feel rock-solid. No animations that distract, no states that confuse, no ambiguity about what happens next.
3. **Minimal input, maximum output** — If a workflow can be one tap, never make it two. The user is usually holding a portafilter in one hand.
4. **Hardware informs design** — OLED display means true blacks. Small screen means ruthless prioritization. Embedded means predictable performance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific accessibility requirements documented. General best practices: sufficient color contrast for critical readouts, touch targets sized for use with wet or greasy hands (from coffee handling), no reliance on color alone for state indication.
